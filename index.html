About

A clinician who reads data, and a methodologist who has seen the ward.

My work sits between two rooms that rarely talk to each other: the clinic, where decisions get made under uncertainty, and the analysis, where those decisions are supposed to be defensible. I trained first as a physician in Pakistan, then as an epidemiologist at Emory, and I am now completing a PhD at Michigan on longitudinal recovery after stroke.

For industry and startup teams, that combination does a specific job. I can tell whether a real-world dataset can actually answer the question being asked of it, design the study so the estimate survives scrutiny, and translate the result for a clinical audience without losing what the numbers can and cannot support. I have built EHR and revenue-cycle pipelines, evaluated a clinical AI system for patient-portal message triage, and written the methods sections that NIH and BARDA reviewers read closely.

The through-line is restraint. I care more about an honest estimate with named limitations than a polished one that performs rigor it does not have.

What I do

Advisory and consulting capabilities

Engagements range from a single methods review to standing advisory work. Each of these is something I have shipped, not a service page.

Real-world evidence & observational studies

Design and analysis on EHR, claims, and registry data. I scope what a dataset can support, choose the design that fits the question, and carry it through to a defensible estimate.

Causal inference & biostatistics

Marginal structural models, inverse probability weighting, g-methods, survival and mixed-effects modeling. Delivered in R, Python, SAS, or STATA with reproducible code.

AI & data products in clinical workflows

Evaluation of clinical NLP and machine-learning systems, graph analytics on messaging data, and REDCap data infrastructure. First-hand experience assessing model behavior against clinical reality.

Regulatory & trial methods

GCP-compliant protocols, IRB submissions, data safety monitoring plans, trial registration, and FDA real-world evidence framing built in from the design stage.

Grant & scientific writing

Lead and co-author on NIH and BARDA proposals and peer-reviewed publications. Methods sections, study rationale, and the cover-letter case for why the work belongs where it is submitted.

Teaching & team mentoring

Graduate curriculum development and hands-on mentoring of analysts, students, and clinical residents in study design, critical appraisal, and data management.

Modeling longitudinal recovery after stroke

Illustrative
The shape of the question my dissertation asks: how functional recovery unfolds over the year after an intracerebral hemorrhage, and how trajectories separate across groups. Toggle a group on or off; hover a point.
Synthetic data for illustration, not study results. A schematic of the mixed-effects trajectory modeling (marginal structural models, inverse probability weighting) behind the BASIC study analysis.
